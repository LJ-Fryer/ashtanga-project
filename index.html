Places of significance - Mysuru -

Kpjay Shala

K. Pattabhi Jois Ashtanga Yoga Shala is the link to the birthplace and source of Ashtanga yoga. This is a very important place to practitioners of Ashtanga and is the only place to truly experience 'Mysore style' practice.

Address:

235, 8th Cross, 3rd Stage, Gokulam, Mysore 570002

What I love about it

You arrive at first light to a relaxed queue outside. As you wait your turn, you watch advanced practitioners blow your mind. You lay your mat down to an unforgettable practice. Before breakfast, your mind and body are nourished and prepared for whatever amazing day you have planned.

Sharath Yoga Centre

The sudden passing of Sri Sharath Jois, leader of Ashtanga yoga, on 11 Nov 2024 was a terrible blow to the Ashtanga community worldwide. He taught thousands of ashtangis and trained almost a thousand teachers in this revered, 77 year old practice.

Address:

Plot No 38-P3 and 39, Hebbal 2nd Phase, Mandya District 571606

Why it is significant

This is an important link to the lineage of Ashtanga yoga, that began in 1948, when Sri K. Pattabhi Jois, beloved grandfather to Sri Sharath Jois, established his Ashtanga Yoga Research Institute in 1948, that quickly garnered an international following that thrives today.

Shravanabelagola Jain Temple

Roughly 2 hours drive from Kpjay Shala, these ancient Jain temples are truly breathtaking (before you even climb the roughly 700 steps). A worthwhile place to appreciate some of the incredible history that the area has to offer. Please enter with utmost respect and appreciation.

Address:

Jinnenahalli, Karnataka 573135

Unforgettable day trip

This is an important link to the lineage of Ashtanga yoga, that began formally in 1948, when Sri K. Pattabhi Jois, beloved grandfather to Sri Sharath Jois, established his Ashtanga Yoga Research Institute in 1948. By the mid 1970’s, Sri K. Pattabhi Jois took his teachings internationally. Today, Ashtanga yoga is practiced worldwide and many styles of yoga can thank ashtanga for their roots

About me

I’ve been practising Ashtanga yoga for over two decades. So much more than just asana (postures), Ashtanga aligns asana with pranayama (breath control), drishti (soft gaze) and bandhas (energy locks) to unlock a practice that nourishes mind and body.
